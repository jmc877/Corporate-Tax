Required Texts and Materials

  • We're going to emphasize learning corporate tax primarily through problems that we cover together in class after the lectures.
  • Corporate Taxation, Examples and Explanations (5th ed), CT E&E, is the primary text for the class. It is available on Amazon, but you also have free access through the FLS Library
  • The Nutshell and Black Letter Outline for Corporate Taxation are useful, and are available for my favorite price (free!) here: WL Study Guides. Here's a concordance for each CT E&E topic from the Nutshell, BL, and Yin & Burke, Corporate Taxation (2nd ed. 2016), which we used in prior years and is a more traditional casebook. I think the book is very solid, but many students did not. You wouldn't be harmed by buying a used version for $20 and reading it along with CT E&E.
  • You'll need access to the Code & Regs. I like CCH, Selected Sections (2022-2023), but you can use the Code & Regs published by West or Foundation. I recommend that you keep it next to you to mark up during class and while doing the problems and readings. Please use the version from your basic Income Tax class or one that was published last year--don't worry, it will be fine. Here a pdf of the IRC ('19-'20). There have been only two significant changes to the domestic corporate tax provisions since 2019.
  • The Code and Regs are available on line at Tax Materials. This is a fantastic free database of fundamental tax materials. On the Supplemental Materials, I show how to make a bookmark that will take you directly to the appropriate code or regulation section. All relevant tax materials are also available on BLaw, WL, Lexis, or Vital Law.
  • For each topic, take a glance at the problems and then read CT E&E and then any additional readings. Use the slides to focus on the important materials that we'll cover during the lectures.
