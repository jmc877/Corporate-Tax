Corporate Tax: Overview

Introduction and Learning Outcomes

Welcome to Corporate Tax! The goal of this course is to acquaint you with the fundamental principles of corporate taxation.


We'll examine the federal income tax laws applicable to corporations and their shareholders. Topics to be covered will include:

  • The formation of corporations and the transfers of assets to controlled corporations
  • The capital structure of a corporation, including debt and equity
  • Distributions from a corporation (e.g., dividends, stock redemptions & liquidations)
  • Special rules applicable to contributions of property with BIG and BIL
  • Special types of corporations (e.g., S corporations, if time)
  • Corporate stock and asset sales
  • Tax-free corporate reorganizations, mergers, spin-offs, and other restructurings


The knowledge gained in this class is extremely valuable in related corporate law classes, such as M&A, corporate reorganizations, introduction to the deal, venture capital, and private equity.


If you have not taken a basic financial accounting course, I highly recommend that you try to take one somewhere somehow before graduation. One option is next door in the business school--the course is 2 credits and is graded on a pass/fail basis. Like finance, accounting is part of the basic grammar of business discourse, and a little knowledge goes a long way. Class Textbook

If Corporate Taxation, Examples and Explanation (5th ed) is published in Jan. 2022, we'll use it as the primary text. If not, you are free to use Yin & Burke, Corporate Taxation (2nd ed. 2016), Burke, Corporate Tax in a Nutshell, or the Schwarz & Lathrope, Black Letter Outline on Corporate Tax.


Problem Sets

For each topic, we'll cover problems that are designed to reinforce the sometimes very difficult materials. I will assign the problems to particular students. I highly recommend that you work on the problems in teams.

Spreadsheet

To do some of the HW problems, you will benefit by using Excel, the world's most popular program. All students have free access to Excel through Fordham. Basic knowledge of Excel is a fundamental business skill, even for lawyers. I've posted some Excel materials on the Additional Resources page.


Laptops
